Buttons

Five button variants cover all interaction needs. Gold is reserved for high-emphasis CTAs. Primary Blue handles most actions. Sizes come in small (36px), default (40px), and large (48px).

Components

Cards

Cards are the primary container for grouping related content. They use subtle borders and shadows, with an optional colored left border to indicate status or category.

Standard Card

Client Health Summary
This card shows the default styling with white background, light border, and medium shadow. Content should be clear and scannable.

Cards with Accent Borders

Growth Metric
Teal left border indicates active, positive, or growth-related content.
Pending Review
Gold left border signals items needing attention or in a pending state.
System Information
Blue left border is used for purely informational notices and updates.
Critical Issue
Red left border highlights errors, failures, or critical items requiring immediate action.
Components

Form Inputs

Inputs use a soft gray background that transitions to white on focus. Focus rings use the OTM Blue with a subtle spread. Error states use red borders with a light red glow.

Authentication and Sign-In Gate

OTM tools authenticate through Google OAuth, restricted to @meetotm.com, never email/password or local accounts. The gate is a centered white card on navy with a single "Continue with Google" button. Identity (name, email, avatar) comes from the provider and is shown in the sidebar user block. See §8.5.

App Shell and Navigation

The standard OTM tool is sidebar-first. The sidebar stacks a logo with product subtitle, a nav with collapsible groups, and a pinned user/account block with Sign out. These are the elements most often missed when a tool is rebuilt, so they are shown here in full. The complete, reusable implementation ships in otm-components.css. On OTM platforms (Venn, CaaS, Mill) the logo block doubles as the Platform Switcher (§5.10). Click .OTM / Venn below to switch apps.
